1. General Animal Behavior & Ecology:

The Study of Animal Behaviour by Geoff Box. This offers an introduction to animal behavior.

Essential Animal Behavior by Graham Scott.

Ecology: Concepts and Applications by Manuel C. Molles Jr.


2. Territorial Behavior:

The Economics of Territorial Behavior in Birds by M. L. Rosenzweig. This paper tackles territorial behavior from an evolutionary and economic standpoint.

On Territorial Behavior and Other Factors Influencing Habitat Distribution in Birds by Robert MacArthur.


3. Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) in Ecology:

Agent-Based and Individual-Based Modeling: A Practical Introduction by Steven F. Railsback and Volker Grimm. This is a guide to the concepts and practical application of ABM in ecology.

Growing Artificial Societies: Social Science from the Bottom Up by Joshua M. Epstein and Robert Axtell. Though more focused on human societies, many principles are applicable to animal simulations.


4. Computer Simulations in Biology:

Simulation for the Social Scientist by Nigel Gilbert and Klaus Troitzsch. Again, while more human-focused, the modeling concepts are widely applicable.

Artificial Life: An Overview edited by Christopher Langton. This book delves into simulating life in a computer context, offering insights that might be valuable for your project.


5. Game Design for Nature Simulations:

The Nature of Code by Daniel Shiffman. While not a traditional biology book, Shiffman's guide on simulating natural systems using code (specifically in Processing) is invaluable for those looking to bring biological principles to a digital medium.

Behavioral Mathematics for Game AI by Dave Mark. This provides insights into the algorithms and mathematics behind game AI, which can be adapted for simulating animal behaviors.


6. Complex Systems & Network Theory:

Complexity: A Guided Tour by Melanie Mitchell.

Linked: The New Science of Networks by Albert-László Barabási. This book explores the theory behind networks, which might be beneficial if you're considering simulating relationships or interactions within your ecosystem.
